Personal finances

May 31, 2025 2-minute read

I’ve ummed and ahhed about what is a good system to track personal finances. For the last three years, I’ve simply used a spreadsheet. I periodically upload my credit card statements as a CSV, categorize the items, and render this in various ways (think nice pie charts and bar graphs). But is this really useful? Sure, it gives me some nice pictures but does it give me any insights into my spending behaviour that actually change that spending behaviour? It’s difficult to give an honest answer, because I can’t reason about the counterfactual (that is, I don’t know how my spending behaviour would be different, were I not to do this).

I’m hesitant to change my methodology as basically any online guide around personal finance insists that knowing where your money is going is the first step. It’s hard to argue with this point. So what can I do to reduce the overhead of this task? I think my method can be improved in two ways.

  1. Regularity: by increasing the frequency with which I do budgeting, the number of transactions I have to process lessens.
  2. Simplicity: I need to simplify the amount of work I do each time. (Currently I have some fancy excel incantation which I use to convert all incoming dates from various credit card providers. I could just label each transaction with the week of the year).
  3. Repeatability: write down the precise step-by-step method involved to (a) find all sources of my spending activity (b) enter and categorize spending activity.
